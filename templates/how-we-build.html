How We Build

Built like a structure, not furniture.

Most chicken coops are built like backyard kits. Ours are built like small outdoor structures — the same materials, the same fasteners, the same construction principles used by professionals who build things that have to survive in Florida for decades.

Our Open-Design Philosophy

Built for Florida. Built to breathe.

Florida has the climate the rest of the country doesn't fully understand. Sustained 90° heat for six months a year. Coastal humidity that doesn't quit. Termites that find anything untreated. Hurricane-force winds that test every fastener you didn't tighten.

A chicken coop designed in Pennsylvania or the Carolinas isn't designed for any of that. Ours is designed for nothing else.

Why It Matters

A coop should outlast the flock that lives in it.

A backyard hen lives 6–10 years. A budget chicken coop, in Florida, lasts about three.

When you buy a Florida Chicken Coop, you're buying a structure that will house generations of flocks — not the first one and then a replacement coop five years later. The materials cost more. The fasteners cost more. The construction takes longer. The result is a backyard investment that pays back every year you own it.
